FY27 Strategy

Where INTL CS is placing
its bets in FY27.

Three bets, each targeting a distinct revenue and customer opportunity. Bet 1 scales what's already working. Bet 2 wraps CS programmes around the payments proposition as it matures. Bet 3 opens a channel — the Accountant — that SER alone can't reach. Together they add up to $12.7M in revenue and a meaningfully stronger competitive position.
Enterprise Alignment
ICS FY27 Input Goals
Every initiative ladders to one of these three goals. Defined at the ICS Forum Q4 2026 ↗
ICS-IG01

Reinventing GBSG End-to-End Customer Resolution

Redevelop human support at scale so well-equipped experts resolve issues quickly, with fewer contacts and less customer effort. Harden feedback loops to eliminate all friction created upstream.

01Eliminate transfers & seams with tiered support and case management
02Equip every expert with tools to resolve issues faster
03Formalise proactive service recovery with named concierges
04Surface cross-functional friction via Signal insights
FY27 Goal: Full Resolution Score 46% → 79%
ICS-IG02

Customer Success at Scale

Transform the cost structure of human intelligence services to deliver better outcomes at materially lower unit cost.

01Stand up AI-powered self-serve & agentic experiences
02Multiply expert productivity with copilot and automation
03Reshape the Expert Network by optimising sourcing, geography, and skill mix
FY27 Goal: Expert AI Help Tool adoption 12K → 15K (+25%)
ICS-IG03

Expert-Led Services

Make expert-led services the engine for customer confidence, ecosystem growth, and lifetime value across bookkeeping, tax, and product support.

01Embed expert guidance inside QuickBooks at critical lifecycle moments
02Drive ecosystem adoption through expert recommendations
03Rethink hiring, incentives, and coaching for an AI-enabled expert workforce
FY27 Goal: Money & Workforce 68K → 119K · Retained QBO 40K → 63K

Big Bet 2
Payments
Supports IG03 · 1 paper
$87K → $349K
In-year to year-3
▾
Wrap G&R and Product Support programmes around the payments proposition as it evolves across international markets. Merchants who complete a first transaction within 30 days retain at 80% vs. 60% for those who don't — a 20-point gap. Currently 70% of new merchants never transact in month one. This bet closes that gap with a phased activation programme combining scale CSM outreach, digital channels, and churn-intent detection. Revenue compounds as Money products mature in the UK.
